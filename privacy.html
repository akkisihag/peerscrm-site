Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 20, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Peers Consultancy, a sole proprietorship registered in India and doing business as PeersCRM (“PeersCRM,” “we,” “us,” or “our”), collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you use our website at www.peerscrm.com, our CRM application at crm.peerscrm.com, and related tools and services (collectively, the “Services”).

We act as a data controller for information about our website visitors and account holders, and as a data processor / data fiduciary's processor for the customer records you upload into the Service on behalf of your own clients. This Policy is designed to meet the requirements of the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act as amended by the CPRA and other U.S. state privacy laws, and India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act).

1. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal information:

CategoryExamples
Account informationName, business name, email address, phone number, and login credentials.
Billing informationBilling contact details and a payment token. Card details are collected and processed directly by Stripe; we do not store full card numbers.
Customer-uploaded dataRecords you or your team enter into the Service about your projects, clients, and subcontractors (“Customer Data”).

2. How We Use Information

  • to provide, operate, maintain, and improve the Services;
  • to create and manage your account and process subscriptions and payments;
  • to provide customer support and respond to your requests;
  • to detect, prevent, and address fraud, security, and technical issues;
  • to send administrative and, where permitted, marketing communications (you can opt out of marketing at any time); and
  • to comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not “share” it for cross-context behavioral advertising as those terms are defined under U.S. state privacy laws.

3. Legal Bases (GDPR)

Where the GDPR applies, we process personal data on the following legal bases: performance of a contract (to provide the Services), our legitimate interests (to secure and improve the Services), your consent (for example, optional analytics cookies and marketing), and compliance with legal obligations.

4. How We Share Information

We share personal information only as follows:

  • Service providers (processors) who help us run the Services — including cloud hosting, payment processing, email delivery, analytics, and customer support — under contracts that require them to protect the data and use it only on our instructions;
  • Legal and safety reasons, where required by law or to protect our rights, users, or the public; and
  • Business transfers, in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, subject to this Policy.

5. Payments & Stripe

We use Stripe as our third-party payment processor. When you make a payment, your card and payment details are collected and processed directly by Stripe under Stripe's Privacy Policy. We receive limited information such as a payment confirmation and the last four digits of your card for reference. We do not store full card numbers on our servers, and Stripe maintains PCI-DSS compliance for card handling.

6. Cookies & Analytics

We use a minimal set of cookies and similar technologies:

  • Essential cookies required to sign you in, keep you logged in, and secure the Services. These are always active.
  • Analytics cookies that help us understand how the Services are used so we can improve them. These are optional and are only used with your consent where required.

You can control cookies through your browser settings, and where required we present a cookie banner so you can accept or reject optional cookies. We honor recognized opt-out signals, including the Global Privacy Control (GPC). We do not use cookies for cross-context behavioral advertising.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Services, and thereafter as required to comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. After account closure we delete or anonymize Customer Data within a reasonable period, and residual copies in routine backups are purged on our standard rotation schedule.

8. International Transfers

We are based in India and our customers are located in the United States and other countries. Personal information may be processed in India, the United States, and other countries where we or our service providers operate. Where we transfer personal data internationally, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent mechanisms as required by applicable law.

9. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, delete, or receive a portable copy of your personal information; to object to or restrict certain processing; to withdraw consent; and to appeal a decision. Residents of California, Colorado, Connecticut, Texas, Virginia, and similar U.S. states have rights under their state laws, and individuals in the EU/UK have rights under the GDPR. Individuals in India have rights under the DPDP Act, including the right to access, correction, erasure, grievance redressal, and to nominate another person to exercise rights.

To exercise any right, email info@peerscrm.com. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law and will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights. If you are an EU/UK resident, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

10. Security

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information, including encryption in transit (HTTPS), access controls, and secure, PCI-compliant payment handling through Stripe. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we work to protect your information and to notify you and regulators of incidents as required by law.

11. Children's Privacy

The Services are intended for businesses and are not directed to children under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us personal information, contact us and we will delete it.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date and, where appropriate, provide additional notice. Your continued use of the Services after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated Policy.
