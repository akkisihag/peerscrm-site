1. Scope & Our Roles

PeersCRM is a customer relationship management platform that helps businesses manage projects, clients, subcontractors, and reporting. We process personal information in two distinct capacities:

  • As a controller — for information about visitors to our Site, prospective customers, and account holders (e.g., your registration details, billing information, and usage data).
  • As a processor / service provider — for information that our customers upload or store in the Service about their own clients, contacts, projects, and subcontractors ("Customer Data"). Our customers control that data; we process it only on their instructions under our Terms & Conditions and applicable data processing terms. If you are a client or contact of one of our customers and have questions about how your information is handled, please contact that business directly.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information you provide to us

  • Account information: name, email address, phone number, company name, job title, and password when you register.
  • Billing information: payment card details and billing address, collected and processed by our third-party payment processor. We do not store full payment card numbers on our servers.
  • Customer Data: contacts, client records, project details, subcontractor information, notes, files, and reports you upload to the Service.
  • Communications: information you provide when you contact support, respond to surveys, or subscribe to updates.

2.2 Information collected automatically

  • Usage data: pages viewed, features used, actions taken, timestamps, and referring URLs.
  • Device and log data: IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers, and crash logs.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: as described in Section 6.

2.3 Information from third parties

  • Integrations you connect to your account (e.g., email, calendar, or storage providers), limited to the permissions you grant.
  • Service providers such as analytics and payment processors.

3. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, operate, maintain, and improve the Services;
  • Create and manage your account and authenticate users;
  • Process subscriptions, payments, and refunds;
  • Respond to support requests and communicate with you about the Services, including service announcements and security alerts;
  • Send marketing communications where permitted by law (you may opt out at any time);
  • Monitor usage, analyze trends, and improve performance and user experience;
  • Detect, prevent, and address fraud, abuse, security incidents, and technical issues;
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms & Conditions.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use Customer Data to train advertising or marketing profiles.

5. How We Share Information

We share personal information only in the following circumstances:

  • Service providers: vendors who perform services on our behalf, such as cloud hosting, payment processing, email delivery, customer support, and analytics, bound by contractual confidentiality and data protection obligations.
  • Integrations: third-party services you choose to connect to your account, per your instructions.
  • Within your organization: with other authorized users on your account, per your account administrator's settings.
  • Legal requirements: when required by law, subpoena, or legal process, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of PeersCRM, our users, or others.
  • Business transfers: in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, subject to this Privacy Policy.
  • With your consent: in any other case, with your direction or consent.

6. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to operate the Services:

  • Essential cookies: required for login, session management, and security. These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: help us understand how the Services are used so we can improve them.
  • Preference cookies: remember your settings and choices.

You can control cookies through your browser settings and, where required by law, through the cookie banner presented on the Site. Disabling certain cookies may affect functionality. Our Services do not currently respond to "Do Not Track" browser signals, but we honor Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals where legally required.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Services, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. When you close your account, we will delete or anonymize your personal information and Customer Data within a reasonable period, except where retention is required by law or for legitimate backup and security purposes, after which residual copies are purged on our standard backup rotation schedule.

8. Data Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information, including encryption in transit (TLS), encryption at rest, access controls, and regular security reviews. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for keeping your account credentials confidential and for the security settings you configure within your account.

9. International Data Transfers

We are based in the United States, and information we collect is processed in the United States and in other countries where our service providers operate. Where we transfer personal data from the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses or other lawful transfer mechanisms.

10. Your Rights & Choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you;
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • Delete your personal information;
  • Receive a portable copy of your information;
  • Object to or restrict certain processing;
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent;
  • Opt out of marketing emails via the unsubscribe link in any message.

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@peerscrm.com. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law and may need to verify your identity first. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

If your information is contained in Customer Data controlled by one of our customers, we will refer your request to that customer and assist them as required.

11. US State Privacy Rights

Residents of California, Colorado, Connecticut, Texas, Virginia, and other states with comprehensive privacy laws may have additional rights, including the rights described in Section 10 and the right to opt out of "sales" of personal information, "sharing" for cross-context behavioral advertising, and certain profiling. PeersCRM does not sell personal information or share it for cross-context behavioral advertising as those terms are defined under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA). California residents may also request details about the categories of personal information collected and disclosed (see Sections 2 and 5) and may designate an authorized agent to submit requests. If we deny your request, you may appeal by replying to our decision email.

12. EEA, UK & Swiss Users

If you are located in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the rights in Section 10 apply to you under the GDPR or equivalent laws. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority. We encourage you to contact us first at privacy@peerscrm.com so we can address your concern.
